Frequently Asked Questions

How does Rusty Degen work?

Players deposit Rust skins into a pot. Your chance to win is based on the value of skins you deposit relative to the total pot. A timer counts down, and once it ends, a winner is randomly selected using our provably fair system. The winner receives items from the pot, minus a site fee.

How do I deposit?

1. Sign in with Steam. 2. Set your Trade URL in your profile. 3. Click "Deposit Skins". 4. Select items from your inventory. 5. Click "Request Deposit Offer" and accept the trade offer on Steam.

Why do I need a Trade URL?

Your Steam Trade URL allows our bots to send you trade offers for deposits and winnings. Find it in your Steam inventory privacy settings.

Is it fair? How is the winner chosen?

Yes, we use a Provably Fair system. A server seed (hashed and shown before round) and a client seed (revealed after) are combined to determine a winning ticket. You can verify this calculation on our "Provably Fair" page.

How do I get my winnings?

Our bot sends a Steam trade offer with your winnings (minus site fee) to your set Trade URL. You'll get a site notification and need to accept the offer on Steam.

What is the site fee/tax?

A small commission (usually 5-10%) is taken from the pot to cover operational costs. This is done by retaining items from the pot.

My deposit offer expired. What now?

If you don't accept the deposit trade offer in time, it becomes invalid. Your items remain in your Steam inventory. You'll need to initiate a new deposit.

Provably Fair System

How It Works

Our system uses SHA256 hashing to ensure transparent and fair winner selection, verifiable by anyone after a round.

  1. Server Seed & Hash: Before a round, a secret Server Seed is generated. Its SHA256 hash is publicly shown.
  2. Deposits & Tickets: Players deposit items. Tickets are assigned based on value (e.g., $0.01 = 1 ticket).
  3. Client Seed: At round end, a Client Seed is generated (often from public round data).
  4. Combined Hash: Server Seed + Client Seed are combined and hashed (SHA256).
  5. Winning Ticket: The final hash determines a number, which is used with the total tickets to pick the winning ticket position.
  6. Seed Reveal: After the round, the Server Seed is revealed, allowing verification.
