About Rusty Degen

The Developer's Story

Welcome! I'm the sole developer behind Rusty Degen, and also a dedicated Rust player with over 7,000 hours logged. This game isn't just code to me; it's a passion.

As a computer science student, Rusty Degen became my advanced project – a way to merge my programming skills with my love for the Rust universe and its unique skin economy. This platform was built entirely from scratch by me.

Being a solo operation means development might proceed at a different pace than larger sites, but it guarantees that every feature is built with personal care and a player's perspective. This project is a testament to applying academic learning to create something tangible for a community I'm proud to be part of.

The Journey

My Rust journey started back in 2018. The game's challenging nature and the intricate skin market quickly captured my interest.

Choosing a project for my studies, I wanted a significant technical challenge connected to my passion. A provably fair jackpot site fit perfectly, involving secure cryptography, real-time interactions, Steam API integration, and engaging frontend design.

From the backend logic to the frontend visuals you see, every part of Rusty Degen is my own work. It's been a challenging but rewarding process.

Community & Transparency

As a player, I understand the importance of trust and transparency, especially in skin gambling. That's why the provably fair system is fundamental to Rusty Degen, ensuring outcomes are verifiable and not manipulated.

I value community feedback immensely. My goal is to build a platform that I, as a player, would trust and enjoy. Your suggestions help shape the future of Rusty Degen.

Thank you for visiting and participating. Your support for this solo project means a lot!

Frequently Asked Questions

How does Rusty Degen work?

Rusty Degen is a Rust skin jackpot site where players deposit skins into a communal pot. When the round concludes (usually via a timer), one participant is chosen randomly through our Provably Fair system to win all the skins in the pot, minus a small site commission. Your probability of winning directly corresponds to the value of your deposited skins relative to the total pot value.

How do I deposit skins?

Begin by logging in via Steam. Click "Deposit Skins" to open a modal displaying your Rust inventory. Choose the items you wish to deposit (meeting any minimum value criteria) and confirm. You will then be guided to send a Steam trade offer to our designated bot, ensuring you include the unique code provided in the trade message. Your deposit is credited once this trade offer is successfully completed.

How is the winner selected?

Winner selection relies on our provably fair cryptographic system. Every $0.01 deposited grants a player virtual "tickets". A secure Server Seed (hashed publicly before the round, revealed after) and a public Client Seed (derived from round data) are combined and hashed (SHA256). This final hash determines a winning ticket number within the total range of tickets issued for the round. The owner of that ticket wins. Visit the "Provably Fair" page for full details and the verification tool.

How do I receive my winnings?

If you win, the items constituting the pot (after the site's commission is deducted) are automatically sent via a Steam trade offer from our bot to the Trade URL registered in your Rusty Degen profile. To ensure successful delivery, please verify your Trade URL is accurate and that your Steam inventory privacy is set to "Public".

Is there a minimum deposit value?

Yes, there's typically a minimum total value required for each deposit transaction (e.g., $1.00, subject to change). Additionally, individual items might need to meet a specific minimum value to be accepted. The deposit interface will indicate if your selected items meet the necessary criteria.

What is your fee?

Rusty Degen applies a small commission (usually between 5-10%) to each jackpot pot. This fee is crucial for covering operational expenses like server hosting, bot upkeep, ongoing development, and ensuring the platform's sustainability and improvement.
